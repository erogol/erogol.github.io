In this post, I like to compute what number of visual instances we observes over time, with the assumption that we visually perceive life as a constant video with a certain fps rate.

Let's dive into the computation. Relying on [1],  average person can see the world with 45 fps on average. It goes to extremes for such people like fighter pilots which is 225fps with the adrenaline kicked in.  I took the average life time 71 years [3] equals to ![2239056000](https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://erogol.com/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_099f735ec44b6e5f87306360500a2662.gif) (2 .24 billion) secs and we are awake almost ![2/3](https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://erogol.com/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_c531884a343e0db77c17c7a33e1b7675.gif) of  it which makes ![1492704000](https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://erogol.com/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_988088006e210531e6ddf5c6e133b7cc.gif) (1.49 billion) secs .  Then we assume that on average there are ![86*10^9](https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://erogol.com/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_29e5e13ede35935734f21215e8247da5.gif)86\*10^9 neurons in our brain [2]. This is our model size.

Eventually and roughly, that means without any further investigation, we have a model with 86 billion parameters which learns from  ![1492704000 * 45 = 67171680000](https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://https://web.archive.org/web/2020/http://erogol.com/wp-content/plugins/latex/cache/tex_9468fbfc39ac2b9a994cdd01c10d2a49.gif)1492704000 \* 45 = 67171680000  almost **67 billion images**.

Of course this is not a convenient way to come with this numbers but fun comes by ignorance ![:)](http://www.erogol.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png)
